I spend my days exploring the emerging patterns of harness engineering — especially how startups and enterprises are putting agents into production — watching where they converge, and distilling those learnings into [Genkit](https://genkit.dev), our open-source harness SDK.

On this blog, I focus on the application side. I read the real harnesses people are building — Claude Code, Goose, Open SWE, the internal agents that companies like Stripe, Ramp, and Coinbase describe on their engineering blogs — and pay attention to the tricks and design decisions that make them behave well. Then I distill those into learnings, with patterns, best practices, and small examples alongside that you can take into your own agents.

Working notes, not grand theory. When I show code, it runs; when I show numbers, they came from a real run.
